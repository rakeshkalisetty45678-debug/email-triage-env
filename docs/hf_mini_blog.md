# Executive Assistant Negotiation Env

## Capability Gap

LLMs still struggle when a task mixes social reasoning, scarce resources, and delayed consequences. A weak assistant can reply politely to one message while accidentally causing a calendar collision, overloading the wrong delegate, or breaking a personal commitment that creates downstream damage.

## Environment

This OpenEnv environment trains an assistant to manage multi-agent executive workflows:

- stakeholders have different incentives and partial information
- meeting slots are scarce and can be double-booked
- delegates have limited capacity and specialized skills
- some requests are better handled asynchronously than synchronously
- protecting personal commitments matters alongside business outcomes

Each episode is a short but meaningful trajectory where the agent must choose whether to reply, delegate, schedule, decline, clarify, or archive, while drafting a message and tracking shared world state.

## Reward Design

The reward uses composable OpenEnv rubrics:

- decision quality
- priority calibration
- coordination quality
- communication quality
- long-horizon trajectory outcome

That gives dense step rewards without losing the delayed signal from overall episode success.

## Early Results

The repo includes an honest baseline comparison between a random policy and a rule-based policy generated by running the environment locally. The rule-based policy outperforms random, which shows the reward function is directionally useful before heavyweight RL training.

## Why It Matters

This setting sits at the intersection of three hackathon themes:

- multi-agent interaction
- long-horizon planning
- personalized task handling

It is a more realistic training target than toy inbox classification because success depends on modeling relationships, incentives, hidden preferences, and future conflicts rather than only labeling a single message.
